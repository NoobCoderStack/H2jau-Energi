Policy & Market Pull

Europe is creating demand for renewable hydrogen. The deployment gap is the opportunity.

H2Jau-Energi helps customers and investors translate regulation, infrastructure gaps and site-level renewable potential into a practical hydrogen storage, fuel-cell backup and surplus offtake strategy.

RED III 42% → 60%

Industry RFNBO target

By 2030, RFNBOs are expected to cover at least 42% of industrial hydrogen use, rising to 60% in 2035.

ReFuelEU 2025 → 2050

Aviation fuel transition

SAF obligations start at 2% in 2025 and rise to 70% in 2050, with synthetic aviation fuel shares starting in 2030.

FuelEU -80%

Maritime intensity reduction

FuelEU Maritime reduces allowed greenhouse-gas intensity stepwise from 2025 to 2050 and includes RFNBO-related mechanisms.

EHO 600 MW

Deployment still needs scale

EU operational electrolysis capacity was around 600 MW in 2025, still far below the 2030 scale required.

European target path

From policy pressure to investable hydrogen projects.

The regulation does not mean every company must immediately buy green hydrogen. It means that specific sectors and supply chains are increasingly pushed toward renewable fuels, RFNBOs and credible decarbonisation pathways.

2025

FuelEU Maritime starts; ReFuelEU Aviation SAF supply obligations begin.

2030

EU renewables target reaches at least 42.5%; Germany targets at least 10 GW electrolysis capacity.

2035

Industrial RFNBO hydrogen target rises to 60%; synthetic fuel and maritime requirements tighten.

2050

EU climate neutrality target and deep fuel transition in aviation and maritime.
