The problem: clean energy decisions are becoming too complex.

Commercial and industrial sites face energy cost risk, CO2 targets and renewable generation volatility. At the same time, batteries, electrolysers, hydrogen tanks and fuel cells must be sized and operated together.

Our solution

A digital decision layer for renewable power, storage and hydrogen resilience.

H2Jau-Energi combines software-based analysis with professional consulting. The tool processes site data, load profiles, generation profiles, storage options, hydrogen pathways and regulatory context.

Policy & Market Pull

Global hydrogen roadmaps are creating a new market for decision intelligence.

Europe, Germany and several Asian countries have hydrogen strategies, roadmaps or emerging policy frameworks. H2Jau-Energi connects these market signals to practical site-level investment decisions.

RED III 42% → 60%

EU industry RFNBO target

By 2030, RFNBOs are expected to cover at least 42% of industrial hydrogen use, rising to 60% in 2035.

Germany 10 GW

Electrolysis target by 2030

Germany targets at least 10 GW electrolysis capacity by 2030, creating strong demand for bankable hydrogen projects.

Asia 2050

Roadmaps are emerging

Malaysia, Japan, Singapore, Korea, Indonesia and Thailand show different hydrogen pathways, from imports to exports, mobility, power and industry.

EHO 600 MW

EU deployment still needs scale

EU operational electrolysis capacity remains far below the 2030 scale required, creating a major investment and project-development gap.
